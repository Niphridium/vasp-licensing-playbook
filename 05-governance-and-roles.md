# 5. Governance & key roles

Regulators license **people and structures**, not just business plans. Get the
roles, independence and fit-and-proper evidence right early — they're slow to fix
later.

## Key roles
- **MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer)** — owns the AML/CFT programme, the
  SAR/STR decision, and the reporting line to the board/regulator. Usually must be
  pre-approved, fit-and-proper, and resident in the jurisdiction.
- **Compliance Officer** — day-to-day regulatory compliance (may be combined with
  or separate from the MLRO depending on size and regime).
- **CISO / Head of Security** — custody key management, infosec, resilience.
- **DPO (Data Protection Officer)** — personal-data handling and privacy.
- **Senior Executive Officer / directors** — accountable management body.

## Fit-and-proper (F&P)
For each key person, expect to evidence:
- Honesty, integrity and reputation (background checks, references, no adverse findings)
- Competence and capability (relevant experience, qualifications, e.g. CAMS/ICA)
- Financial soundness
- Residency / time commitment where required

Keep an F&P file per person: CV, certificates, ID, background checks, declarations.

## Independence & three lines of defence
- **1st line** — the business owns and runs controls.
- **2nd line** — compliance/risk sets policy and monitors.
- **3rd line** — independent audit tests effectiveness.

The MLRO must have genuine authority and a direct line to the board — not be
buried under commercial pressure. Examiners probe this.

## Common pitfalls
- One overloaded person wearing MLRO + Compliance + CISO hats in a real business.
- An MLRO who is non-resident or clearly a figurehead.
- No documented escalation path from analyst → MLRO → board → regulator.
